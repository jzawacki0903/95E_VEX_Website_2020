Building Tips:

Throughout our collective experience, we have learned many useful tips and tricks to use while building a robot. Here are a few of the most useful, in our opinion.

Gear Ratios

Using gear ratios can allow you to adjust the speed or torque of an subsystem in motion without changing the speed of the motor. When powering a chain of gears, powering a small gear that drives a larger gear is a torque gear ratio, while powering a larger gear that drives a smaller gear is a speed gear ratio. When using subsystems that may be lifting or pushing heavy loads, a torque gear ratio is very useful, while a speed gear ratio is best used for something that needs to be fast, without pusing heavy loads.

Pillow Bearings

When creating a drive, you generally want to create a drive that has enough torque to play defence on other robots, but with enough speed to traverse the field in a matter of seconds. One way you can increase the speed of your robot without compromising on torque is to use pillow bearings instead of regular bearings. Pillow bearings are made out of a different type of plastic than regular bearings, resulting in pillow bearings having much less friction. Pillow bearings are mounted either on top of, or on the underside of, your chassis, to where the motor is in a position perpendicular from the ground to power it.
