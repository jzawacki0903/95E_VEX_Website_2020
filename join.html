Joining a VEX Robotics Program

There are over 11,000 different teams from schools and organizations that participate in the VEX Robotics program, and it is relatively simple to join one!

CEC TriBots:

If you go to a high school in the Coweta County School System and want to join VEX Robotics, all you need to do is wait for the announcement on the intercom announcing the start of the VEX Robotics season. The announcements will provide details such as where and when the information meeting is, and which room at the CEC to meet in. This meeting will also explain the try out process and requirements.

Middle School:

Middle schools around the world run a VEX program called VEX IQ. VEX IQ is a middle-school level robotics competition that uses smaller robots with a different game than the high school and college level competitions. If you are interested in joining a VEX IQ team at your middle school, check the clubs page on your school website for a VEX Robotics team, or approach a teacher to get the club started!

High School:

High school teams around the world run the VEX program known as VEX EDR. The CEC TriBots teams participate in VEX EDR, so we have more first-hand experience when it comes to this game. VEX EDR is a high-school level robotics competition which uses bigger robots and a different game from the middle school or college level competitions. As above with the middle school, joining a high school team requires going to your local high school website and finding VEX Robotics under the clubs page.

College:

Colleges and Universities around the world also can participate in a VEX Robotics program known as VEX U. VEX U has teams not only building a robot larger than both VEX EDR and VEX U, but also building a smaller robot to participate with the larger one. Combining the necessity for two robots with the harder game, VEX U is definitely the hardest VEX program and suitable for college level. Join VEX U at your college or university by expressing interest wherever clubs are advertised!
