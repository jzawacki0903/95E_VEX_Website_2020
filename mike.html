Team 95M's Robot (Fernando)

Picture Updated 11/29/2018

“Team 95M’s robot, occasionally named Fernando for no discernable reason, contains many systems and subsystems. The chassis of the robot is shaped similar to a ‘H.’ The drive train is made of two large omni-wheels, each powered by a torque motor, on both sides of the chassis, and a large traction wheel that is unpowered but also located on both sides. The drive train was designed to allow the robot to park at the end of each match to earn extra points for their alliance, while also allowing ease of motion and driving throughout the rest of the match. There is a six bar lift with a cap flipper attached to the end of it to score caps during matches. The cap flipper is powered by a servo motor and has an external gear ratio to allow it to turn 180 degrees and remain level. Team 95M is currently working on building a linear puncher to launch balls at flags. An intake of chain and rubber flaps will bring in the balls from the field and send them down a ramp to the puncher.” -Janie Edgar 10/23/18
