Our Team:

We are the CEC TriBots, a division of teams that participate in the VEX Robotics High School Competition. We are four teams made up of students chosen from the three local high schools that go to the Central Educational Center, a school that teaches the core courses, as well as electives such as Pre-Engineering, Web Design, and other electives that allow students to give a taste of what that career may look like for their future. We compete in competitions in the state of Georgia in hopes of gaining tickets to the State and World Championships. More importantly, we compete to learn about robotics and engineering and to have fun.

Our Story

The CEC TriBots have been participating in VEX Robotics since 2011, starting with the competition known as Gateway. The name TriBots gets its origins through the fact that the CEC TriBots consists of students from the three high schools in the school system, hence Tri in TriBots. The T and B in TriBots are both capitalized to honor the memory of a VEX member, Thomas Broadwater, who passed away in a car accident some years ago.

Our Other Programs

The CEC TriBots compete in multiple robotics competitions outside of VEX Robotics each year. For the past few years, the CEC TriBots have competed in the BEST Robotics competition. The CEC TriBots participates in both the Marketing and Robotics aspects of VEX Robotics, with moderate success in each. In the 2017-18 BEST Competition, our company, CEC Hydrodynamics and Rescue, won first place in the CADD challenge and third place in the Marketing Presentation, with slightly lower scores in everything else. New this year, the CEC TriBots will also be participating in this year's MATE ROV competition, which involves building robots that participate in underwater challenges.
