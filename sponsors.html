Our Supporters:

The CEC TriBots organize and participate in many community outreach programs in order to get more people involved and aware of STEM opportunities.

Parents/Guardians

The parents and guardians are big helpers and supporters to the CEC TriBots. Besides providing support and cheer for the CEC TriBots at competitions, the parents and guardians also help by paying the membership fees which allow us to purchase new parts and register for more competitions each year. Donations can also be made throughout the year, with the majority being food donations to take to competitions.

The Browns

Scott and Pamela Brown are the official sponsors of the CEC TriBots Robotics Team. Besides ordering new parts, registering us for competitions, and providing transportation for the CEC TriBots, they also make all of the food that we take to competitions and custom make all of the CEC TriBots apparel that we wear to competitions. Mr. and Mrs. Brown also provide a sounding board for ideas involving the robot, without explicitly telling us to do or not to do something, and to challenge us learn from our ideas.

Onion Sales

At the end of each VEX Season, the CEC TriBots also sell freshly grown Vidallia onions, a Georgia state crop. This fundraiser is where the majority of the funds for new parts and competitions come from, as prices can add up and the onion fundraisers are always a great way to get the CEC TriBots' name out, as well as raising money for the program.

Outside Supporters

Two local engineering companies also provide support and donations to the CEC TriBots. The German Engineering company, Grenzebach, as well as Kason Industries support the CEC TriBots in the form of grants and other needed materials. Recently this past fall, Kason Industries sent Mr. Brown a $20,000 grant to spend on various engineering machines and devices for classroom and CEC Tribots' use.
